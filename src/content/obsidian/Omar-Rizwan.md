From his website:

I also [tweet](https://twitter.com/rsnous) a lot about computers these days, on topics including:

* wanting [provenance](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1416236409115930627) everywhere, throughout the computer, whether you're programming or [looking at data](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1415452111765082112) or [typing](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1147891162033709056) or [taking a screenshot](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1130910375795277824) or whatever; knowing how the thing was made / the soil that it grew in / the context in which it's actually valid
* [getting](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1425915072761634817) [functionality](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1419792020059475969) ['for free'](https://twitter.com/search?q=from%3Arsnous%20%22for%20free%22\&f=live) from [the architecture of the computer system](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1018570015866384389)
* how end-user programming can simplify & [improve](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1290657813249892354) systems, empower users, [remove the need for 'configuration'](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1055443539218202625)
* [against](https://twitter.com/maxkreminski/status/1130911420135043073) [separating](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1262867035828023296) 'content' and 'presentation' / what does ["serving a function"](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1191605982075351040) really mean?
* the role of [dread](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1257165715238313986) in [computing](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1257169965175541761); weird, [intense](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1251810672544845826), [consequential](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1276158622565912579) feelings that are produced by seemingly-incidental details in how the software works
* how we should lean into existing user 'folk practices' like [taking photos](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1113157229207740416), [taking screenshots](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1092912875339866113), and using hacked-together spreadsheets
* [programming environments](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1293340325637795841) as [culture](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1078457221011378176); knowledge (of programming, of reading and writing, etc) as something that exists [in a community](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1261851188422533121), not a property of one person's head; how the social ([imitation](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1063484525760544768)) drives what people want to learn and make
* how the grain of underlying materials and [the tractability of doing certain things](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1163105743148421121) ends up silently guiding what people do (not necessarily a bad thing -- [it can be a source that you can learn from!](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1191604752523509761))
* speculation on how software could have more [physicality](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1296295224499560448), spatiality ([1](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1297325001670971393), [2](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1251249279718420481), etc), [vitality](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1251808189131001857), [how a 'toy' might be better than an 'app' or a 'game'](https://twitter.com/rsnous/status/1257801104030482432)
